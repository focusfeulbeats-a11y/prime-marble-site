FAQs

Porcelain or ceramic — what's the difference?

Porcelain is denser and less porous, making it a stronger choice for floors and wet areas. Ceramic is often lighter and more budget-friendly for walls.

Can you tile over existing tiles?

Sometimes, if the substrate is sound and flat enough. In most cases we recommend removing old tiles for the best long-term finish.

How long does a bathroom retile take?

A standard bathroom typically takes 2–4 days depending on waterproofing, trims and grout curing time.

Do you supply the tiles?

We can source tiles through our suppliers, or install tiles you've already chosen and purchased.
