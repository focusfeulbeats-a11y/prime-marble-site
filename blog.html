How to Care for Marble

Marble is a beautiful natural stone that adds elegance to any space, but it does require some care to maintain its appearance over time. With the right routine, your marble surfaces can look stunning for decades.

Daily care:

  • Wipe surfaces with a soft, damp cloth after use
  • Use pH-neutral stone cleaner — never acidic or abrasive products
  • Blot spills immediately, especially acidic liquids like lemon juice or wine
  • Use coasters and trivets to protect from heat and staining

What to avoid:

  • Vinegar, lemon, or bleach-based cleaners
  • Abrasive scrubbing pads or powders
  • Leaving standing water on the surface
  • Placing hot pans directly on the stone

Annual maintenance: We recommend professional resealing once a year to maintain stain resistance and protect the stone's natural beauty.

Designing a Kitchen with Stone

Stone worktops are the centrepiece of any luxury kitchen. The right choice of material, edge profile, and layout can transform your space from ordinary to exceptional.

Material selection:

  • Carrara Marble: Classic, affordable, versatile grey veining
  • Calacatta Marble: Bold, dramatic veins for statement kitchens
  • Engineered Quartz: Consistent colour, highly durable, low maintenance
  • Granite: Extremely hard-wearing, unique natural patterns

Edge profiles: A pencil edge offers a clean, modern look. An ogee edge adds traditional elegance. A waterfall edge creates a dramatic, continuous flow down the sides of an island.

Layout tips: Plan your veining flow across seams for a cohesive look. Consider bookmatching for feature walls or splashbacks. Allow for adequate overhang on islands for seating.

Bathroom Stone: Best Options

Not all stones are created equal when it comes to wet environments. Choosing the right material and finish for your bathroom ensures both beauty and longevity.

Best stone choices:

  • Marble: Stunning but requires sealing. Best for vanity tops and wall cladding.
  • Quartz: Non-porous, no sealing needed. Ideal for busy family bathrooms.
  • Limestone: Warm, natural look. Requires regular sealing in wet areas.
  • Porcelain: Extremely durable, water-resistant. Great for floors and walls.

Finishes: A honed (matte) finish is less slippery for floors and hides water spots better than polished. Polished finishes create a luxurious, reflective look on walls and vanity tops.

Sealing: Natural stone in bathrooms should be sealed professionally before use and resealed annually to prevent water absorption and staining.

Choosing the Right Tile Size

Tile size has a significant impact on the visual feel of a room. Large format tiles create a seamless, spacious look, while smaller tiles add texture and detail.

Large format (60x60cm+):

  • Creates fewer grout lines for a cleaner, more modern appearance
  • Makes small rooms feel larger
  • Requires perfectly flat walls and floors for best results
  • Ideal for open-plan kitchens and large bathrooms

Medium format (30x30cm to 60x60cm):

  • Versatile and forgiving on slightly uneven surfaces
  • Works well in most residential spaces
  • Good balance between visual impact and practicality

Small format / Mosaic:

  • Great for feature walls, niches, and shower floors
  • Adds texture and visual interest
  • More grout lines mean more maintenance
  • Excellent for creating patterns and borders
